📖 1. Poem Text (ପୂର୍ଣ୍ଣ କବିତା)

Note: The "Wheels on the Bus" part is an introductory song, not the main poem.

Stanza 1
The seat is blue in colour.
The tyres and pedals are black.
Sometimes fast, sometimes slow,
But my bicycle is always on track.
Stanza 2
To keep my bicycle moving,
All I need is some air.
I put on my little helmet,
And go happily everywhere.

⚠️ Bell sound is not given in the poem; see Question 4 in Q&A section.

📝 2. Odia Summary (ସାରାଂଶ)

ଏହି କବିତାଟି ଏକ ଛୋଟ ପିଲାର bicycle (ସାଇକେଲ) ବିଷୟରେ ।

  • Bicycle seat blue colour (ନୀଳ) ।
  • Tyres (ଟାୟର) ଓ pedals (ପାଦଥିବା) black colour (କଳା) ।
  • Bicycle ଟି sometimes fast, sometimes slow (ବେଳେବେଳେ ଶୀଘ୍ର, ବେଳେବେଳେ ଧୀର) ଚାଲେ, କିନ୍ତୁ always on track (ସବୁବେଳେ ଟ୍ରାକ/ବାଟରେ) ରହେ ।
  • Bicycle move (ଚଳାଇବା) ପାଇଁ air (ପବନ) ଦରକାର ।
  • Child put on helmet (helmet ପିନ୍ଧେ) safety (ସୁରକ୍ଷା) ପାଇଁ ।
  • Happily everywhere (ଖୁସିରେ ସବୁଆଡ଼େ) bicycle ଚଳାଏ ।

🔍 3. Stanza-wise Explanation (ଓଡ଼ିଆ ବ୍ୟାଖ୍ୟା)

Stanza 1 (seat, tyres, pedals, speed)
  • The seat is blue in colour. – Bicycle seat blue colour (ନୀଳ ରଙ୍ଗ) ।
  • The tyres and pedals are black. – Tyres (ଚକ) ଓ pedals (ପାଦଥିବା) black (କଳା) ।
  • Sometimes fast, sometimes slow, – Bicycle sometimes (ବେଳେବେଳେ) fast (ଶୀଘ୍ର), sometimes slow (ଧୀର) ।
  • But my bicycle is always on track. – But (କିନ୍ତୁ) my bicycle always (ସବୁବେଳେ) track (ସଠିକ ରାସ୍ତା/ପଥ) ଉପରେ (ଉପରେ) ଥାଏ (ଥାଏ) ।
    Artha: Bicycle sabubele nirdista rasta anusarana kare.
Stanza 2 (moving, air, helmet, everywhere)
  • To keep my bicycle moving, – Bicycle moving (ଚଳମାନ) ରଖିଲେ (ରଖିବାକୁ) ।
  • All I need is some air. – ମାତ୍ର (ମାତ୍ର) need (ଦରକାର) air (ପବନ) ।
  • I put on my little helmet, – ମୁଁ (ମୁଁ) ମୋ (ମୋ) ଛୋଟ (ଛୋଟ) helmet (helmet) ପିନ୍ଧେ (ପିନ୍ଧେ) ।
  • And go happily everywhere. – ଏବଂ (ଏବଂ) ଖୁସିରେ (ଖୁସିରେ) ସବୁ ଜାଗା (ସବୁଆଡ଼େ) ଯାଏ (ଯାଏ) ।

❓ 6. Questions & Answers (ପ୍ରଶ୍ନର ଉତ୍ତର)

Answer in complete sentences – Odia translation included for help.

1. What is the poem about?

The poem is about a child's bicycle.

Odia: Eh kabiṭā tiye gotie chota pila bicycle bisayare.

2. What colour is the bicycle?

The seat is blue, and the tyres and pedals are black. So the bicycle is blue and black.

Odia: Bicycle ra seat neeli, tyres o pedals kaḷa. Tenu bicycle neelī o kaḷa rangara.

3. How many times is the word 'I' used in the poem?

Only one time – in the line "I put on my little helmet".

Odia: Sabubele – "I put on my little helmet" line re thile.

4. How does the bell sound?

The bell is not mentioned in the poem. However, generally a bicycle bell sounds "tring, tring".

Odia: Kabita re bell katha nahin. Sadharanata bell 'tring tring' sound kare.

5. What colour is the seat?

The seat is blue. (Seat neelī.)

6. What is the colour of tyres and pedals?

They are black. (Tyres o pedals kaḷa.)

7. What does the bicycle need to move?

It needs air. (Bicycle move karibā pāin air/pabana dorkār.)

8. Why does the child put on a helmet?

For safety, to protect the head while riding. (Muṇḍa surakhya pain helmet pindhe.)

🧠 7. Visual Memory Development Technique (Memory Activity)

Goal: Poem picture mind're "eye camera" re capture kara.

Whole Memory: Take the full poem picture in your mind. Now answer:
(i) Which paragraph tells about keeping bicycle moving?
→ Stanza 2
(ii) Which lines tell about seat and tyre?
→ Stanza 1: "The seat is blue…" and "The tyres and pedals are black."
(iii) Which paragraph tells about bicycle moving?
→ Stanza 2 ("To keep my bicycle moving…")
(iv) Which paragraph tells about bell and pedal?
→ Bell not in poem; pedal in Stanza 1
Part Memory: Without seeing, recall these words from the picture:
move, colour, sometimes, track, air, helmet, everywhere
Then use your index finger to "point" to where they appear in the mental image.

🎨 8. Extra Practice (Suggestions)

  • Draw a bicycle with blue seat, black tyres and black pedals. Add a helmet on the rider. Label the parts in Odia:
    seat = asaana (ଆସନ) tyre = taar (ଟାୟର) pedal = paadatha (ପାଦଥିବା) helmet = śirṣa-trana (helmet/ଶିରଷ-ତ୍ରାଣ)
  • Recite the poem with actions: roll hands for wheels, pretend to pedal, touch head for helmet.
  • Practice writing the new words 3 times each.
  • Make a paper bicycle craft and colour it.

🌀 9. Twist Questions (ଘୁମୁରା ପ୍ରଶ୍ନ)

Think carefully! These aren't direct "copy from the poem" questions.

Q1. (Colour Twist) If you could repaint your bicycle, what one new colour would you choose for the seat? Why?

Open answer – student's choice with a reason. Example: Red, because my shirt is red.

Odia: Tume seat ra colour blue badalile, kouthi colour karibe? Kana pain?

Q2. (Air Magic) Imagine one day you open the tyre and all the air goes out – what happens?

The bicycle cannot move properly. It will be very hard to pedal, and the ride will be bumpy (flat tyre).

Odia: Jodi tyre ru sabu paban palaey jaae, bicycle ki heba? – Bicycle ṭhik se chaliparibani; pedal mariba kathina, jhatka lagiba.

Q3. (Never in the Poem) What colour do you think the little helmet is?

Guess – no right/wrong. Motivates imagination. Eg: Maybe red, like a strawberry. Or white, like safety.

Odia: Kabitare helmet ra colour kahini. Tume bhabucha helmet ra colour kana?

Q4. (Bell Sound Detective) If you were the poet, what two words would you write for the bell sound?

"The bell on my cycle goes tring, tring, tring…" or "The bell rings 'ting-ting' all the way."

Q5. (Track Mystery) If there is no road, only a big green field – is the bicycle still "on track"?

Bicycle can move on the field, but it's not a "track". The poem says "on track" meaning following a proper path. On a field, there's no fixed track, so it might wander. Also harder to ride on grass.

Odia: Rasta nahin, kebala padiya. Bicycle 'track' re chalipariba ki?

Q6. (Opposite Day) Rewrite with opposite meaning: "Sometimes fast, sometimes slow"

"Always the same speed" or "Never changing speed" – clever answers encouraged.

Q7. (Little Helmet Puzzle) Why "little" helmet? Can a father wear it?

It's little because the child is small. A father cannot wear it; it's too small. The word "little" shows it's made for a child.

Q8. (Emotion Hunt) Find the word that tells us the child's feeling while riding.

happily (go happily everywhere).

Odia: Ditiya stanza re pila khusi achhi boliki kete sabda? – 'happily' – mane khusire.

Q9. (Rhyme Twist) Find a word that rhymes with "black" from the poem.

track (black – track: /blæk/ and /træk/ – they share the "ack" sound).

Q10. (Riddle Time – Parts of Bicycle)

• I am round and black, I love the road. Without air, I become flat. → Tyre
• You push me down and up with your foot, and the bicycle moves forward. I am not the wheel. → Pedal
• I sit on top, you rest on me; I am blue in the poem. → Seat
